Damages/effects:

Typhoon Paeng caused numerous landslides, flash floods, infrastructure destruction, and flooding, with the wind playing a minor role. This typhoon produced PHP 17.6 billion in damage and an estimated 119,532 deaths. As a result, a huge number of people will be forced to seek refuge and temporarily dwell outside their damaged homes caused by the typhoon. Furthermore, this storm caused power outages and interruptions to livelihoods, particularly in agricultural regions. Typhoon Paeng produced emotional distress, service interruptions, and probable resource exhaustion in the Philippines, necessitating relief and restoration efforts by government agencies, non-governmental organizations (NGOs), and the international community.
